Growtopia

Growtopia is a free-to-play, massively multiplayer online (MMO) 2D sandbox game where players can create unlimited worlds, build anything they can imagine, and interact with a global community.

01 • Economy Design

Analyzing Season Pass Progression

The Challenge

Tasked with auditing and optimizing the season pass to heavily boost key KPIs including purchase conversion, completion rates, revenue per buyer, and retention.

Solution & Result

Adoption rate of the pass went up from 5-7%. A/B testing with past configurations proved the new configuration won significantly.

trending_up

Execution & Methodology

Analyze Drop-off Points & XP Sources

Pulled telemetry data to identify exactly at which tiers players were stopping (e.g., assessing churn spikes at specific tiers). Documented where players were sourcing their XP and if they were resorting to monotonous grinding due to mathematical efficiency.

Community Sentiment vs. Math

Cross-referenced perfect mathematical balancing against player perception on Discord and Reddit. Realized that complaints of a "grind" often translates to: "the rewards aren't worth the elapsed time."

Rebalancing The Pacing Curve

Tiers 1-5 Early Game: Extremely fast acceleration to give an immediate dopamine hit, act as onboarding, and drive premium conversion.
Tiers 6-20 Mid Game: Smoothed out the curve, shifting XP weight toward Daily/Weekly Quests to create healthy session lengths and drive returning DAU without burnout.
Tiers 21+ Late Game: Increased friction for the chase, ensuring the exclusive rewards precisely matched the effort required.
02 • Content Design

Owning a Key LiveOps Asset

The Challenge

Out of 10+ items designed, I was tasked with designing the Hero item for the Growpass November 2025 within a strict 2-week deadline. The goal was to decode what drops player motivation in an open economy and synthesize that into a high-performing asset.

Solution & Result

A Good Impact Release: The November pass successfully hit its projected financial numbers and KPIs.

Technical Ownership: When shipped implementations of particle VFX didn't match the design, I directly dove into the game's XML codebase to fix it myself, ensuring the final product delivered on the vision perfectly and on time.

Methodology

search_insights

Rapid Visual Research

Analyzed 1,000+ existing items to crack the specific visual markers (VFX, rarity cues, animation styles) that signaled "high value" to players, allowing me to speak the game's aesthetic language immediately.

psychology

Pop Culture & Integration

Research showed successful items often relied on relatable pop-culture references or new mini-gameplay system changes. Navigating the 2-week scope, I leaned into pop-culture targeting our core demographic.

group_work

Cross-Discipline Alignment

Defined visual direction via moodboards and documented edge cases. This gave the Art team clear constraints paired with creative freedom, minimizing iterative loops.

03 • Economy Design • Tools: Excel

Balancing The Drop Rates

The Challenge

Balancing an open-market game economy is extremely volatile. For the November event drops, items were traded by players for "World Locks" or "Diamond Locks". Thus, an item's value isn't just determined by its utility, but heavily dictated by its scarcity relative to the total currency in circulation.

The Logic & Formula

task_alt Target Definitions: Found the Hard Cap and Soft Cap targets first (Exactly how many of each item should exist in the entire game after a certain time period).
task_alt Saturation Point: Factored in player base size and historical data to calculate true market saturation.
task_alt Item Sink Modifiers: Is the item consumable, permanent, or transmutable? If the item has no way to leave the economy, its drop rate must be curbed significantly with mathematical modifiers.
task_alt Opening Frequency: Calculated by synthesizing parameters like Cost of Entry, global Gem Inflation, and designated Time-Gating limits.
