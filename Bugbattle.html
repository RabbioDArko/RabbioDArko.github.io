01 • System Design

Prototyping Core Wave Mechanics

The Challenge

Tasked with establishing the fundamental combat rhythm, the goal was to create an engaging enemy spawn system and interconnected weapon upgrade logic that scales seamlessly across numerous short sessions.

Solution

Drafted modular concept documents mapping base weapon stats, synergies, and detailed progression, allowing for rapid iteration in early prototypes.

architecture

Execution & Methodology

Enemy Spawn Systems

Designed a wave structure entirely parameterized by the number of enemies, specific enemy IDs, and spawn timestamps. This quantitative approach ensured encounters felt orchestrated rather than randomly chaotic.

Pacing The Gameplay Loop

Phase 1 Preparation: Lower threat encounters allowing players to establish their baseline, gather initial resources, and plan their build.
Phase 2 Main: Sustained engagement where enemy density begins to stress-test the player's weapon synergies and tactical positioning.
Phase 3 Climax: High-density, high-threat scenarios that truly challenge the culmination of the player's session upgrades and skill.

03 • Quality Assurance

Playtesting & Balancing

Validating the Loop

Mechanics on paper don't always translate to fun. To ensure the progression felt rewarding, the game underwent intensive playtesting and balancing.

The Testing Scope

Internal Feedback: Conducted structured group sessions across internal studios to gather baseline data on the new weapon logic.

Public Validation: Successfully presented the game at major public events like IGDC, observing raw player reactions to the wave phases.

Feedback Integration

track_changes

Actionable Tracking

Meticulously tracked each piece of qualitative feedback and linked it to a corresponding quantitative change in the balancing parameters. This prevented reactionary changes and kept iterations focused.
