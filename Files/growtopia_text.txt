Asking questions like, at what specific tier are players stopping? Is there a massive churn spike at Tier 15? If so, why? Is the XP gap between Tier 14 and 15 too high, or is the reward at Tier 15 terrible? Audit XP sources : Grow is a game with multiple liveOps event in a single month. I had to document Where are players getting their XP? Are they grinding one specific, boring activity because itâ€™s the most mathematically efficient? Community Sentiment vs. Math: I started looking into Discord and Reddit. Sometimes the math is perfectly balanced, but the perceived grind felt awful. If players say "it takes too long," it usually means "the rewards I'm getting along the way aren't worth the time I'm spending." Calculate the "Total Time to Complete": Map out exactly how many hours/actions are required to hit the max tier. Rebalance the Pacing Curve: Early Game (Tiers 1-5): Goal was to make it extremely fast. Give them a dopamine hit immediately. This acts as onboarding to the season and drives premium conversion. Mid Game (Tiers 6-20): Smooth out the curve. Shift the XP weight heavily toward Daily and Weekly Quests rather than raw grinding. I wanted to create healthy session lengths (preventing burnout) and drive returning DAU. Late Game / Chase (Tiers 21+): Goal was to increase the friction but ensure the rewards match the effort. Solution &amp; Result Adoption rate of pass went up from 5-7% , A/B testing with past configuration proved the current configuration won. [Content Design] Owning a key liveops asset Challenge In total I have designed 10+ items and shipped 3 items but there is one item I would like to talk about. I was tasked with designing the Hero item for Growpass November 2025. I had a strict 2 week deadline to go from zero to final design. [Economy Design] Balancing the Drop Rates Designing a economy for an open-market game like Growtopia is a challenge of its own. I was responsible for balancing the November event drops which means fine tuning the drop rates of items that were dropping from events from November. In this game each item are traded for "World Locks" or "Diamond Locks", the value of an item isn't just determined by its utility, but by its scarcity relative to the total currency in circulation. Well I cannot give the exact calculation due to NDA restraints but I can explain the work process and the logic.
