01 • System Design

Clan Event Progression System

The Challenge

Our studio needed to elevate the performance of our Clan Events, specifically targeting low session times and player engagement metrics. We identified that while the core gameplay was solid, players lacked a compelling long-term incentive to stay logged in during the event window.

The Results

The design was successfully greenlit and moved into full production, with engineering validating the adapted backend approach as a highly resource-efficient solution.

Approach & Execution

To solve this, I designed a symbiotic system connecting specific player actions to a tiered reward structure. My process focused on two key areas: the User Experience (UX) flow and Technical efficiency.

Designing the Progression Loop

I conceptualized a Metagame Quest System linked to a "Season Pass" style progression.

  • Behavioral Design: Mapped user flows to ensure that high value in-game actions directly contributed to quest progress.
  • The Reward Structure: Structured the Season Pass with a finite number of tiers to create reachable, satisfying goals.
  • Visualizing the Flow: Created wireframes and flowcharts to demonstrate action -> notification -> reward claim -> unlock.

Technical Adaptation

Rather than building a backend architecture from scratch, I investigated our studio's legacy projects. I identified a compatible quest backend from a previous title and adapted its logic to fit the constraints of our Clan Event. This approach significantly reduced estimated engineering time.

02 • Technical & Content Design

Designing a New Beast for Halloween

The Challenge

Beasts were the main element of play in our game, and thus the major part of content was driven by unlocking new beasts. I was tasked with designing two new beasts for the Halloween event. We were limited by the availability of VFX artists, meaning we had to tinker around existing VFX to create new Beast Spells.

Solution & Impact

Positive Community Feedback: The Halloween event had a demonstrably positive impact on the players, refreshing the meta and adding strong thematic immersion during the holiday schedule.

Execution Strategy

analytics

Meta Analysis

Analyzed gameplay around current beasts to identify stale patterns. Designed the spell kits and identities to disrupt these loops, specifically creating a synergizing shield spell for beasts sharing its nature.

magic_button

Technicality (VFX)

When it came to VFX, we had to implement it ourselves. I tinkered with the existing VFX in Unity Engine to ensure it worked cohesively with our new beast and its animations.

code_blocks

Technicality (Scripting)

Beast scripting flow was done in JSON. With the support of the Senior Game Designer, I got my hands on the scripts and created all the bespoke spells required, fine-tuning the balance history and math.

03 • Narrative Design

Transforming Onboarding (FTUE)

I was given a task to boost D0 engagement with our clan event. The existing First Time User Experience (FTUE) lacked fiction, stakes, and emotional hooks—players knew how to click the buttons, but they didn't understand why they should care.

help Context

Redesigned the FTUE to immediately answer: What has happened? What should I do next? Why does this matter?

movie Rhythm

Structured onboarding as a rhythm of: Narrative Beat -> Gameplay Action -> Narrative Payoff, preventing text dumps.

psychology Inspiration

Taking inspiration from best-in-class titles like Cookie Run: Kingdom to keep players deeply engaged via story.

04 • Economy Design

Balancing The Power Economy

The Philosophy

While working on the beast balance for this project, my main goal was to ensure the "Power Economy" remained healthy. It required a rigorous mathematical anchor to prevent power creep while ensuring rarities felt appropriately powerful.

Rarity Balancing Formula Example:

Common Beast 1.0× Base Stats
Legendary Beast 1.5× Base Stats
* Requires 2.5× XP to level (XP Sync)
calculate

The Logic Flow

category Define Archetype: Established clear classifications (Tank, Warrior, Rogue, Mage) to assign stat weights correctly.
anchor Beast Power Anchor: Treated "Beast Power" as the summary metric and mathematical limit (e.g., Target BP at Level 100 = 50,000; the sum of all weighted stats cannot exceed this).
moving The Progression Curve: Customized progression curves depending on beast goals and rarity tiers.
savings The Economic Cost: Utilized XP as the primary economy sink, scaling required XP non-linearly against stat acquisition speed.
